# How did we design and make the house?

> Read this to gain insight into our design and construction process for the model houses featured in our experiment

___

## Designing the house

- Firstly, we started by modelling the houses on onshape, a CAD software for the web
- For the walls, we used *box joints* to ensure a uniform angle throughout the house (attached below)
- For our window covers and door, we designed custom 3D parts to cover them, printing them in white PLA
- After that, we exported the .dxf file to Techsoft V3
- For all parts of the house, we used 4mm plywood and then laser cut them
- To simulate insulation, we decided to use a 3mm thick foamboard

## The construction of the house

- After the houses were laser cut and the window/door covers were printed, we started construction
- We found it relatively easy to assemble the frame of the house, thanks to the box joints
- To hold them in place, we used PVA glue and glued them between the individual teeth of the sides
- After that, we then used a strip of tape to further strengthen the build along the edge of the house
- After measuring the sides of the interior walls, we cut out specialist sizes of foam to attach to the walls
- We then used PVA glue to stick the foam onto the walls
- For the window and door covers, they fit snugly in place. To strengthen it, we then ran it over with a strip of tape

## What about our instruments?
> In instruments, we mean the temperature sensor and the means of heating/cooling

- We attached the BME temperature sensor onto the floor for every house, positioned at the middle of the floor
- For the heater, we attached it onto one of the sides of the house
- The fan was also attached on a opposite side of the house
- Keep in mind, **the positioning of all instruments were kept the same for every test and every house**
